how to write an introduction

You may want to start your Introduction by describing the problem you are trying to solve, or the aim of your work . it is not the right way

grammar and writing skills

  • tense pairs 时态（一般现在时✓，现在进行时）一般现在时表肯定
  • signalling language
    • sentence connection
      • overlap
      • pronoun or pro-form(this method)
      • not finish the sentence, join it with semicolon or relative clause (which i.e)
      • use signalling sentence connector such as therefore or however
        • cause: as ,because, since
        • result: therefor, consequently, hence, do not use so(it is informal)
        • contrast/difference: however, whereas, but
        • unexpectedness
          • although, even though, though
          • despite, in spite of, regardless of, notwithstanding
          • nevertheless, however, yet, nonetheless, even so
        • addition
          • in addition, moreover, furthermore, apart from that/which
          • also, secondly, in the second place, what is more
  • passive active use 主动与被动
    • where referring to we, don’t use I, or it, t is also common to use the passive instead of we, especially in the central ‘report’ section, or use dummy subject to take the place of I or we
      • it is known that (passive mode)
      • This article describes an algorithm for clustering… (omit we)
  • paragraphing 分段

to find out quickly what it is about and where the various pieces of information are located

writing task: build a model

In Sentence 1 ‘Polylactide (PLA) has received much attention in recent years due to its biodegradable properties, which offer important economic benefits.’ ==the writer establishes the importance of this research topic.==

use Present Perfect tense or Present Simple tense

In Sentence 2 ‘PLA is a polymer obtained from corn and is produced by the polymerisation of lactide.’ ==the writer provides general background information for the reader.==

In Sentence 3 ‘PLA has many possible uses in the biomedical field1 and has also been investigated as a potential engineering material 2,3’ the writer does the same as in Sentences 1 and 2, but in a more specific/detailed way, ==using research references to support both the background facts and the claim for significance.==
